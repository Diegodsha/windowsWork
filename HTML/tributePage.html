Valentino Rossi

A six-time MotoGP champion

Here's a time line of Valentino's career:

He has won nine world titles in four categories: 125cc ( 1997 ), 250cc ( 1999 ), 500cc ( 2001 ) and is a six-time MotoGP champion ( 2002 , 2003 , 2004 , 2005 , 2008 and 2009).). He has participated in the top category, with the three great motorcycling teams of his time: Honda , Yamaha and Ducati , being since 2013 , a Movistar Yamaha MotoGP rider .

  • 1979 - He was born in Urbino on February 16, 1979. His parents are Graziano Rossi and Stefania Palma. He has a brother (on his mother's side), Luca Marini , also a motorcycle racer, and a sister, Clara Rossi (on his father's side).
  • 1990. - From a very young age he lived in Tavullia , where he began his life in motor sport racing in karts supported by his father Graziano . He won the regional championship in this year.
  • 125 cc category - In 1996 he made his debut in the world championship in the Aprilia-AGV team and after ten races in which he obtained a third place as the best result, he obtained his first victory in the Grand Prix of the Czech Republic of Motorcycling in Brno.
  • 250 cc category - In 1999 Rossi is champion after winning 9 races leaving 48 points behind Tohru Ukawa and 100 points behind Loris Capirossi, which is worth his promotion to the highest category.
  • 500 cc and MotoGP
    The years with Honda
    - In 2000 he made his 500 cc debut riding the Honda NSR500, finishing second in the championship 49 points behind Kenny Roberts Jr.
    - In 2003 Rossi won 9 races in the same season. It was his fifth world title and the third in a row in the queen class. At the end of this season it became known that Rossi had signed for Honda's rival brand : Yamaha , due to the differences that HRC had with Rossi. At the 2002 Valencia GP, Barros, who had been riding the NSR500 all year, got on a 4T for the first time and beat Rossi.
  • Champion with Yamaha (2004-2005) - In 2004 Rossi, after leaving Honda, the winning bike, raced for Yamaha, winning 9 races and taking the title.
    - In 2005 he again won the world motorcycle championship in the race held at the Sepang circuit , on September 25 of that year. He won 11 of the 18 races contested, added 16 podiums, winning 367 out of 425 possible points and retired only once, in the Japanese Grand Prix.
  • The drought of results (2006-2007) - For the 2007 season he renewed with Yamaha and had very irregular presentations: he won in Jerez, Mugello, Assen and Estoril (where he dedicates the victory to Colin McRae who died the day before the race), he finishes second in Losail, Shanghai and Montmel√≥ (Barcelona) , but tenth at Istanbul and sixth at Le Mans.
  • The return of the champion (2008) - In 2008 he returned with Yamaha but with Bridgestone tires instead of Michelin . He lost in the first three races and Stoner, Pedrosa and their teammate, the rookie Jorge Lorenzo took the podium. Then win 3 consecutive races.
  • Ninth world title (2009) - In 2009, again with Yamaha, after a third place at Mugello, Rossi finished first. On June 27, at the Dutch Grand Prix in Assen, Valentino clinches the 100th victory of his career and celebrates it with a giant banner with photos of each of his 99 previous victories. He finishes second at Laguna Seca, triumphs in Germany and is fifth after a crash in Indianapolis.
-- Currently, he is the rider with the most podium finishes (235) in the history of the Motorcycle World Championship , and has also achieved the highest number of victories (89), podiums (199) and fastest laps (76) in MotoGP .
